Color Palette

When I became the Creative Director of GEN-ZiNE, I nurtured a space for collaboration to ensure brand cohesion and inspire collective creativity.

For each zine, I had designers pitch color palettes to represent each zine’s theme —spectrum and renaissance. This step is essential to visual cohesion in print design and digital design. These are the color palettes for each zine.

Collaborative Moodboards

Following the color palette selection, each design team made moodboards. By engaging in this exercise, the designers were able to form working relationships and align their unique visions for each deliverable.

Rough Drafts

Rough drafts are critical to any deliverable. This stage of our production process —both in designing for print and digital— ensured the delivery of high-quality final deliverables that expertly reflected the brand priorities.

Final Deliverables

Key Takeaways

  • Visual motifs are underutilized yet a great way to create cohesion in assets for the web, social media, and print

  • For multi-step, multi-medium projects, deadlines need to be met & reinforced

  • Fonts are super important to establish consistent branding

  • If you have a team with multiple designers, make it clear where they can be creative and where they need to follow the style guide
