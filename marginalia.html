Moodboard

This project's conception started at an unconventional step: the moodboard. Alongside the magazine's founders, I created a moodboard to act as a visual guide throughout the magazine production process prior to creating a style guide, color palette, or even a logo.

Style Guide

With the moodboard complete, I used it to guide me and construct a style guide, with everything instructed from font type, to text wrap settings, column settings, and export instructions.

For so much of the style guide, the magazine needed a solid font type that would add visual interest without distracting from the zine's visual aesthetic: realism and collages. To achieve this result, I selected the sans-serif Forma DJR Banner by David Jonathan Ross. It is similar to Helvetica, while unique in its subtle curves and rounded corners. Then, to set our zine apart, we have an off-white color as the background color, alongside a mid-toned blue, a dark navy, and a light blue. These design choices ensure the magazine's readability without a screen's eyestrain and a unique visual identity when printed.
