We Don't Care. Music

On-Boarding

Since this was a client-designer relationship, the biggest priority for me was to understand how I could help my client both with my visual skills and software expertise. Basically, I want my designs to be useful and convenient for my clients.

During this process, I wanted to resolve any potential obstacles that could arise after my partnership with the organization. In this situation, I needed to create social media assets that were easily editable and looked good next to each other. In order to achieve these objectives, I created labeled, editable Photoshop documents using the software's Smart Object feature.

Brand Guidelines

In this project, I was hired to refresh the company's branding as well as their social media marketing. In order to achieve this task, I needed to know their target audience and how they planned to reach it. In other words, I needed to fully understand their branding priorities.

Since this brand showcases new musical talent, in which the musical talent has their own marketing aesthetic, I needed to develop a brand that wouldn't visually clash with lots of other aesthetics. In order to achieve this branding priority, I created black and white brand materials.

Key Takeaways

  • Solve the client's current & potential problems —this might involve labeling or giving off-boarding Photoshop tutorials

  • Less colors can sometimes be more, especially in regards to social media

  • When rebranding, design a formal logo, a small logo for a profile picture, and maybe a medium-sized logo
